About Me

Looking Ahead

While I don't consider myself a traditional reader, I know that I am still actively engaging with words and texts every day. I am excited to use this class in order to strengthen my writing skill and possibly find some ways to connect with reading that is fun and natural to me. I'm looking forward to learning with everybody this semester!

My Literacy Spaces

While I am not really into reading books for fun, I do still read and write a lot in other spaces. One of the main things I enjoy learning right now is investing. For example, I follow a Reddit community about investing called Financial Planning. In those spaces, reading and writing show up in ways I actually enjoy. I really like to read the problems other people have and the tips the community gives which I then take into my own consideration for my investments. I also like to ask questions about investing advice and love to learn from experienced investors.

My Reading & Writing Experiences

Honestly, I have never been the type of reader who reads for fun. Everyone has childhood favorites such as Harry Potter, or Percy Jackson, but the only book I really enjoyed was Diary of a Wimpy Kid. Reading outside of school was never something that I ever wanted or thought to do. The only times I read a novel or articles for research projects is when it is assigned as homework from schools.

While I don't read very much for fun, it doesn't mean that I dislike stories. I actually really enjoy them a lot, just in different forms like movies, shows, and even in games. I would describe myself as a reader who reads more practical stuff like information, not really to get lost in a good story.

As a writer, I often struggle with finding the right words and how to word certain things, but I know that I can express myself better when I just start writing rather than overthinking. I hope in this class I can learn new strategies that make writing feel less like an "assignment" and more like a tool I can use for future uses. I also want to unlearn the idea that reading has to mean sitting down for hours and having to force myself to read a long book, as there are many ways to read and engage with texts.

What I Like Talking About

Something that I love to talk about is sports, specifically baseball right now. I usually talk about baseball with my friends, mostly in person, but sometimes through texts or platforms like Discord. These interactions might not seem like your traditional reading and writing, but they are. These conversations include communication, interpretation and expression just digitally and socially.
